Top 10 Widest Ships in the World

The width of a ship is also known as its beam, and it is measured at the widest point of the hull.

The beam affects the stability, manoeuvrability, and capacity of a ship.

The wider the beam, the more stable the ship is in rough seas, but also the more difficult it is to navigate through narrow channels or ports.

The widest ships in the world are usually oil tankers, gas carriers, or crane vessels, which need a large beam to carry heavy or bulky cargo.
