Understanding Nicollette Sheridan’s Finances

Acting Career Highlights

Sheridan’s breakout role came in 1986 when she joined the cast of Knots Landing as Paige Matheson. This role made her a household name and earned her critical acclaim.

However, her defining role was as Edie Britt on Desperate Housewives, which she played from 2004 to 2009. This role not only brought her fame but also a lucrative salary of $175,000 per episode, contributing significantly to her net worth.

Television Movies and Miniseries

Beyond regular TV series, Sheridan starred in several television movies in the late 80s and 90s, such as Deceptions and Lucky Chances. Her versatility and consistent performances kept her in demand, and she continued starring in TV movies well into the 2000s, including Haven’t We Met Before? and Deadly Betrayal.

Film Career Contributions

While her film career isn’t as extensive as her TV career, Sheridan still made notable appearances. She starred in comedies like Noises Off and Spy Hard, alongside legends such as Michael Caine and Leslie Nielsen. These roles, while fewer, still added to her financial portfolio.

Legal Battles and Financial Impact

Sheridan’s career faced a hiccup with a high-profile lawsuit against ABC and Marc Cherry, the creator of Desperate Housewives. She alleged wrongful termination and battery, seeking $20 million. Real Estate Investments

In December 2008, Sheridan and Michael Bolton purchased a 7,500 square foot mansion in Hidden Hills, California, for $1.375 million. Sheridan kept the home post-breakup, and its value soared to $16 million by 2023.

Such smart investments have played a pivotal role in maintaining and increasing her net worth.

Personal Life and Relationships

Sheridan’s personal life has been equally eventful. She was married to Harry Hamlin and later to Aaron Phypers. Her relationship with singer Michael Bolton was highly publicized, and despite two engagements, they never married.
